New Medical Facility Set to Serve Residents of Sto. Tomas and Neighboring Towns

Sto. Tomas, [Date] â€“ Residents of Sto. Tomas and neighboring towns will no longer have to travel far for medical care, as the Jose B. Lingad Memorial General Hospital (JBLMGH) is poised to open an additional building in this municipality.

This development follows the signing of the contribution deed between the municipal government of Sto. Tomas, JBLMGH, and the Department of Health (DOH). The opening of the JBLMGH Ambulatory Surgical and Multi-Specialty Center is scheduled for December of this year, marking a significant improvement in local healthcare services.

JBLMGH received the lot and building from the Sto. Tomas municipal authority to extend its operations in the area. The structure, built in 2019 by former Fourth District Representative Juan Pablo "Rimpy" Bondoc and Mayor John Sambo, was initially planned for the town's community hospital. However, its launch was hampered by the COVID-19 pandemic, which resulted in lockdowns that prevented it from serving its intended purpose. During the pandemic, the structure served as a quarantine center.

Mayor John Sambo expressed his commitment to improving the town's healthcare services, stating, "Malaki ang magiging tulong nito para maisulong ang serbisyong pangkalusugan sa aming bayan dahil may sarili na kaming ospital na magbibigay ng kalidad at libreng serbisyo."

Fourth District Representative Anna York Bondoc commented on the significance of the JBLMGH extension building, which will serve residents around the Fourth District, including Bulacan. She also noted that it is JBLMGH's first extension building outside its compound in the City of San Fernando. "We're very excited about this because we desperately need it," she said. "We are all aware that the Fourth District is prone to floods, and we cannot go when it occurs; however, with the launch of the JBL expansion in Sto. Tomas, we will have easier access to its services."

JBLMGH's medical director, Dr. Monserrat Chichioco, emphasized the hospital's services, which will include clinics for medical consultations, radiography, laboratory, dental services, and pharmacy. "Sa tulong ng donation na ito, mas mailalapit natin ang serbisyong medikal mula JBL papunta sa ating mga bayan-bayan para mas marami tayong matulungan," she stated. DOH Undersecretary Eric Tayag and Central Luzon Director Corazon Flores were also present for the gift deed signing.
