Commuter Fares Increase for Traditional and Modern Public Utility Jeepneys

SAN FERNANDO CITY — Traditional and modern public utility jeepneys (PUJ) will charge an additional peso in commuter fares starting from October 8, 2023.

This decision comes after the Land Transportation Franchising and Regulatory Board (LTFRB) approved a "provisional" fare hike of ₱1 on Tuesday.

The Pangkalahatang Sanggunian Manila and Suburbs Drivers Association Nationwide, Inc., Alliance of Transport Operators and Drivers Association of the Philippines, and Alliance of Concerned Transport Organization have been asked to submit a supplemental petition regarding their proposal for a ₱5 fare increase for the first four kilometers and an additional ₱1 for subsequent kilometers.

The LTFRB has clarified that it has approved a ₱1 fare increase for the first four kilometers but not for subsequent kilometers.

This ₱1 increase is considered "temporary" until the LTFRB makes a final decision on the groups' primary petition.

As a result of the temporary rise, the minimum fare for classic PUJs will be ₱13, and for contemporary jeepneys, it will be ₱15.

The LTFRB has urged public transportation companies to continue providing discounts to senior citizens, people with impairments, and students, as required by law.
